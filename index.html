We Have Been Telling Stories Since The Beginning Of Our Species

Be it history, mythology, or folklore, stories have always been a fundemental part of our communication with eachother; and in one form or another fantastical stories have always been a part of that.

Why Fantasy Matters

Fantasy stories are a fundemental learning tool.

"Fantasy is vital for the human mind. It begins as the psychological process by which a child learns to fill the gaps between knowledge, reality and experience, and becomes a vital adult coping mechanism"(The Guardian, The Importance of Fantasy).

How It's Been Used
The Epic of Gilgamesh
One of the first written stories, it teaches that death is inevitable, so you should make use of the time you have.
The Orestia
A trilogy of Greek plays written in the 5th century BC. These tragedies used the horrible fates of many of the characters to teach the audience that blood feuds never ended well.
The Parables of Christ
Whether or not you believe that Jesus was real, the parables he told are a very well known example of using fantastical stories to teach others moral lessons.
The Epic of Beowulf
An extended poem about an incredible hero fighting a variety of monsters and a dragon. It used the actions of its hero to emphasize the virtues of Courage, honor, and Loyalty.
The Hobbit
One of the most iconic examples of the fantasy genre, the works of J.R.R. Tolkien have since inspired many stories. The Hobbit specifically was a childrens story and had an ending to teach kids about facing your fears and friendship.
Aladdin
This beloved Childrens movie includes and abundance of magic and uses this to tell an entertaining story about the virtue of honesty.

Key Benefits of Fantasy

Stimulates the Imagination

Fantasy stories can help foster creativity and grow the minds of the readers, especially those of children.

Entertaining

Fantasy can be fun to watch and help the audience get some time away from the stress of their lives.

Sense of Communtiy

Readers who enjoy the same stories often find eachother and make lasting friendships, in part, because of the similar stories they consume.

Teach Moral Lessons

Either intentional or not, all stories have a moral lesson that can be useful for teaching others and getting it's audience thinking about the world around them.
